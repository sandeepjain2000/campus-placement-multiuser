Based on my analysis of your deck and current market research, here are high-impact improvements organized by priority. The campus recruitment platform market is valued at $2.15B in 2026 and growing at 6.8% CAGR , so positioning matters significantly.
🎯 Critical Missing Elements
1. Lead with Traction, Not Just Vision
Your deck reads like a product spec. For a B2B SaaS at this stage, investors and pilot customers want proof points. Add a "Traction & Validation" slide before the product deep-dive with:
Pilot institution names (even if LOI-stage)
Employer partners committed
Student count in sandbox
Any revenue or pipeline metrics
Key metrics: CAC, time-to-hire reduction, admin hours saved
"At seed stage, investors expect customer validation, product usage, revenue signs, and pipeline" 
2. Quantify the Problem
Your "fragmentation" slide lists symptoms but lacks scale. Replace the icons with hard numbers:
"78% of TPOs spend 15+ hours/week on manual coordination"
"Employers repeat onboarding with 40+ campuses per season"
"Students miss 30% of deadline updates due to channel fragmentation"
Cite industry data: AI is cutting hiring cycles by 40%  — frame PlacementHub as the enabler of this efficiency for the campus niche.
3. Add a "Why Now" Slide
Missing entirely. The market is at an inflection point:
Campus recruitment platforms are shifting from on-premise to cloud SaaS 
Skills-based hiring is overtaking degree-centric filtering 
AI-powered screening is now table stakes, not a differentiator 
Your rules engine and partnership-gated workflow address this shift — but you need to name the timing explicitly.

5. Competitive Landscape Slide
You mention Handshake, Symplicity, JobTeaser implicitly but never address them. Add a 2x2 matrix:
X-axis: Generic vs. Campus-specific
Y-axis: Job board vs. Full workflow OS
Place PlacementHub in the top-right: campus-specific + full lifecycle governance. Your moat is the rules engine + partnership gate + multi-tenant architecture — say this explicitly.
6. Business Model Slide
Completely absent. Define:
Pricing: Per-student/year? Per-campus subscription? Freemium for employers?
Unit economics: ACV, expansion revenue from alumni module
Revenue expansion path: mentorship fees, employer sponsored posts, analytics premium
🎨 Content & Visual Improvements
7. Simplify the "Feature Deep-Dives"
You have ~40 features listed across slides. This overwhelms. Instead, use 3 pillars:

8. Make the "Founder" Slide Credible
Current slide is a bullet list. Instead:
Add a founder-market fit story: "After 30 years staffing 500+ campus drives, I watched the same Excel crash every season"
Add advisor logos or key relationships
Move this slide earlier if you're the primary credibility driver at this stage
9. Student Journey → Visual Flow
Replace the 9-step numbered list with a horizontal swimlane diagram showing:
Student actions (top lane)
System validations (middle lane: CGPA, deadlines, rules)
Admin visibility (bottom lane)
This visualizes your core value: automated governance.
10. End with a Clear "Ask"
Your final slide says "Immediate next steps" but doesn't specify:
Funding ask (if pitching investors): "Raising $X for pilot expansion"
Pilot commitment (if pitching colleges): "Seeking 3 anchor institutions for Fall 2026"
Partnership targets (if pitching employers): "Onboarding 10 enterprise partners by Q3"
